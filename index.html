A word from Pia

"Adamawa women have always carried more than their share. They wake early, sleep late, and love their families without asking for credit. This network is my way of saying: I see you. I stand with you. And together, we will build an Adamawa that stands with you too."

Dr. Patience Ishaku Abbo

Our Vision

A state where every woman counts

The women of Adamawa have held families, markets, and whole communities together across generations — quietly, without recognition. Pia Na Sia says that changes now.

Ward by ward. Group by group. All 21 LGAs. We are not waiting for permission to matter.

When one woman rises, she carries the whole community with her. That is the only kind of rising that lasts.
Dr. Patience Ishaku Abbo
Seven Focus Areas

Here is what we fight for

01
📚
Education and Literacy
Schooling for the girl-child and a second chance for the woman who missed hers. No daughter of Adamawa stands outside the door of knowledge.
02
💰
Economic Empowerment
Skills, access to capital, and support so every woman can grow her own trade and feed her family on her own terms.
03
🤰
Maternal and Child Health
Safer pregnancies, healthier children, better care. No mother loses her life giving life.
04
🛡️
Protection of Women and Girls
An end to violence and harmful practices. Safety is the right of every woman in Adamawa, not a privilege.
05
🎙️
Leadership and Civic Participation
Adamawa women belong in every room where decisions are made. We mentor, we push, we lead.
06
🤝
Support for Widows and Vulnerable Women
Welfare, vocational support, and real community care so no widow or displaced woman carries her burden alone.
07
📱
Digital and Technological Inclusion
Digital literacy, online business tools, and the future of innovation. Our young women belong in technology too.
